⚙ How to collect scores across your team
Each CXO scores themselves. There are two practical approaches — choose the one that fits your session format:
Option A — Live session input
Each person calls out their self-score for each trait as the group works through the table together. The facilitator (or any team member) enters the scores in this shared view. Recommended for in-person sessions where open discussion of each trait is part of the process.
Option B — Pre-session individual scoring
Each CXO completes the printed or Excel pre-assessment individually before the session. Scores are transferred into this tool at the start of Arc 1 for immediate team DNA computation. Recommended when anonymity or independent reflection is preferred before group discussion.
Scoring guide: Rate yourself on each trait using the 1–5 scale. Score 1–2 = Low (the low-end description applies to you), 3 = Balanced (you operate in the middle range), 4–5 = High (the high-end description strongly applies to you). Score your honest default behaviour, not your aspirational self. Only roles present and scored are included in the team profile.
What Your Team DNA Means for the Strategy BUILD Process
Your aggregate personality profile creates predictable patterns in how this team will navigate strategy design. These are not opinions — they are structural implications drawn directly from your scored profile. Address the risks before they become Burning Platforms in later lenses.

Arc 2 — Intelligence · 0:55–1:40
Why does Heart & Mind Alignment determine execution outcomes?
Alignment of hearts and minds is the ignition point of execution — the moment where strategy transitions from a document describing intent into a system capable of producing movement. This arc establishes why this matters operationally, introduces the Commitment vs Compliance distinction that will shape your leadership architecture, and equips you with the ACE-IT behavioural framework and the Emotional Climate Thermometer as your live diagnostic instruments.
The Ignition Point Logic Compliance vs Commitment ACE-IT Anchor Behaviours Emotional Climate Thermometer
The Ignition Point of Strategy Execution
A strategy document contains strategic potential — direction, intent, and ambition. Execution begins only when an individual decides: "This matters to me. I am willing to invest energy in making this succeed." Until that moment occurs across the organisation, strategy remains theoretical. Aligning Hearts and Minds activates the human energy required to convert intention into action. Three logics explain why this lens determines execution outcomes:
Potential → Kinetic Energy
Strategy at rest is potential energy only. No system executes itself. The human decision to invest is the conversion point from intention to movement.
Commitment vs Compliance
Compliance produces supervised output. Commitment produces self-driven momentum. Only alignment achieves the latter — and only the latter survives when leadership attention shifts.
The Iceberg Below the Waterline
What is visible (behaviour) is shaped by what is emotional (how the strategy is experienced), which is shaped by what is cognitive (how it is interpreted). Leaders who address only the visible layer change nothing durable.
The PEC Funnel
Platform → Emotion → Cognition. The execution platform people stand on determines their emotional state. Their emotional state determines the quality of their thinking. Stabilise the platform first.
📊

Diagnose where your organisation's alignment failure is occurring

The PEC Funnel shows the causal chain between execution context and decision quality. When organisations struggle with poor decisions, they typically intervene at the Cognition layer (retraining, new processes, better information). But the root cause is usually at the Platform layer — the social, structural, and emotional conditions in which the thinking is happening. Fix the platform first.

Arc 3 — Extrapolating · 1:40–2:25
Where are the alignment hot zones across your organisation?
Extrapolating is the discipline of locating where in your organisation alignment between hearts and minds is required, where it is beginning to fail, and where the symptoms of misalignment are most visible. These zones consistently appear across seven domains — the COMPASS. Your task is to map your organisation's current heat level in each domain and identify whether you are managing a High Flammable zone or already operating from a Burning Platform.
COMPASS — 7 High Flammable Zones Burning Platforms Distinction Hot Zone Mapping — Your Organisation
High Flammables vs Burning Platforms: High Flammables are leverage points where human interpretation and emotional state have a disproportionate impact on performance. When alignment is strong, they amplify performance. When alignment is weak, they escalate. Burning Platforms are High Flammables that have remained misaligned long enough for the consequences to become visible and costly. At a Burning Platform, staying still is more dangerous than change — alignment work becomes corrective, not developmental.
Your task: For each of the seven COMPASS domains, expand the zone, read the organisational signal narrative, and rate the current heat level in your organisation (1 dot = emerging risk, 5 dots = burning platform). Add your own observations about where misalignment is showing up. This map will feed directly into your Converging Zone work in Arc 4.
Arc 4 — Converging Zone · 3:05–3:30
How does your CXO team create the Panoramic Convergent Zone?
The Converging Zone transforms functional expertise into coordinated leadership judgment. Without convergence, your organisation receives multiple interpretations of strategy — causing instability across the seven COMPASS domains. With convergence, your executive team operates as a single leadership system. This arc is the collective intelligence moment of Lens 1: you move from individual self-awareness to a shared map of how your roles jointly stabilise the organisation's alignment terrain.
