Strategy Foundation Diagnostics

Strategy is the output of iterative thinking, logic and process to determine what, why, how and when an organisation creates, delivers and sustains value to its stakeholders while navigating the forces within its operating environment.

Strategy2Results® Master Definition
The granular breakdown — distilling the critical elements
Each concept in the master definition carries strategic weight. Understanding what each means — and what breaks when it is missing — is the foundation of all work that follows. Output · Iterative · Thinking & Logic · Process
The nature and mechanism of strategy itself — what it is and how it works as a system.
The 4 Ws — What · Why · How · When
The four strategic dimensions that define every coherent strategy. Weakness in any one creates structural incompleteness across the DIG framework.
Value Triad — Creates · Delivers · Sustains
The three layers of value that every strategy must design explicitly. Partial chains produce partial results — and usually undetected leakage.
Stakeholders · Navigating · Forces · Operating Environment
The external and relational context within which every strategy must be designed and navigated. These are not background conditions — they are active variables.
The Strategy Engine — three interlocked components
Three components must run simultaneously for strategy to be both sharp and durable. They have distinct roles. Confusion between them is itself a strategic failure.
Critical Architecture — How They Stack
The three components form a layered system. Failure in any one cannot be compensated by strength in another.
Layer 1 — Foundation
Thinking Logic
Determines whether conclusions are sound
The quality of cognitive reasoning that drives every strategic judgement. Get this wrong and everything built on top is sophisticated nonsense.
Poor logic → well-structured, confident, wrong decisions
Layer 2 — Scaffolding
Process
Organises thinking into repeatable steps
Without process, good thinking stays in people's heads. Process converts private insight into shared, testable, documented strategic positions that survive leadership change.
Poor process → inconsistent decisions that restart every cycle
Layer 3 — Motion
Iterative Nature
Revisits and improves decisions over time
Even sound logic well-structured will become stale. Iteration is what keeps strategy alive — testing assumptions against reality and revising in response to evidence.
Poor iteration → stale strategy defended as settled doctrine
The Real Insight — Where most organisations fail
What most organisations do
Focus heavily on Process (frameworks, templates)
Add some Iteration (reviews, strategy days)
Largely ignore Thinking Logic — the quality of reasoning
What the result looks like
Well-structured, regularly reviewed… bad decisions
Strategy that is internally coherent but fundamentally wrong
Confidence that is not grounded in sound logic

"Thinking Logic determines whether we are right. Process ensures we are consistent. Iteration ensures we get better. Most organisations only invest seriously in Process — which is why most strategy fails not from lack of effort but from errors of logic, not errors of data."

Causal Logic Chain — input to output
The end-to-end causal chain: how inputs become strategy. Each stage is a necessary condition for the next. Weakness at any stage propagates forward as strategic failure.
